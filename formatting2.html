Jumpy Jaws

The page going back to the top every 10 seconds didn't help with that big block of text either, did it? That automatic page refresh was designed to simulate the issue that screen readers have so often where they lose focus and jump back to the top of the page. Better structure and formatting help at least recover from this. Bet you were wishing for a few headings, or at least some line breaks to split up that big block of text and tell you where you left off. That's how screen reader users feel when navigating a page that keeps losing focus and has too few structural elements like headings for the screen reader to jump to quickly. There is no way to skim read with a screen reader. The closest they can come is using headings to navigate quickly. However, this is not the same thing at all. There is no way to quickly and briefly scan a text. This means that massive blocks of text are especially annoying with screen readers. Doing things such as finding quotes in the middle of blocks of text Which visual readers find easy is an impossibility with screen readers without reading a text a bazillion times.



Pop-Ups and Ads

And then there's the giant unicorn. It makes the text very hard to read, blocking most of the screen. This is similar to the way that pop-ups start talking over the screen reader, making it difficult to hear what it is saying. They also often block progress through the website as they are sometimes impossible to navigate past with a screen reader. These are issues screen readers commonly face. Most ads are not accessible, often rendering the website unusable. This is true for many articles, but especially in every recipe website on Google, at least the top ones.
